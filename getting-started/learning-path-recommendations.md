## Overview

Not every reader needs to start at page one and read every word in sequence. This page maps the handbook's content to the most common roles and experience levels, so you can find your optimal entry point and follow the thread that delivers the most value for your specific situation. The one universal recommendation: complete the Foundations section before jumping into Series 1, regardless of your experience level. It takes less than two hours and prevents the vocabulary gaps that slow down the hands-on phases.

## Key concepts

* **Foundations-first principle** — The five Foundations pages establish the vocabulary and mental models that every Series 1 chapter assumes. Practitioners who skip Foundations consistently misinterpret platform constructs because they are mapping them to the wrong conceptual model. Two hours upfront saves ten hours of confusion later.
* **Phase dependency in Series 1** — Series 1 is a sequential build. Each phase produces configuration that the next phase consumes. Phase 1 creates the Entity and Authority Document structure that Phase 2's Risk Assessments require. Phase 2 produces the Risk Register that Phase 3 links controls to. Jumping into Phase 3 without Phases 1 and 2 leaves you configuring records that have no data relationships.
* **Parallel reference use** — The [GRC Terminology Glossary](../foundations/grc-terminology-glossary.md), [Regulatory Frameworks Overview](../foundations/regulatory-frameworks-overview.md), and Resources section are designed to be consulted in parallel with any other section, not read linearly. Bookmark them.

## Suggested pacing

The handbook is built to be consumed over two to four weeks at a few hours per week, not in a single session. The following pacing works well for most practitioners:

**Week 1 — Foundations**
Work through all five Foundations pages. Set up your PDI and activate the GRC plugin (see Prerequisites & Tools). Goal: fluent vocabulary and a working instance ready for Series 1.

**Week 2 — Series 1, Phases 1 and 2**
Phase 1 covers instance setup, importing authority documents, and configuring Entities and Profiles. Phase 2 covers building Risk Statements, the Risk Register, and running Risk Assessments. Goal: a populated risk register with assessed risks linked to authority documents.

**Week 3 — Series 1, Phases 3 and 4**
Phase 3 covers Control Objectives, Controls, evidence collection, and attestation workflows. Phase 4 covers executive dashboards using Performance Analytics and the IRM risk posture report. Goal: a complete, reportable IRM build.

**Week 4 — Industry Use Cases and Advanced Topics**
Apply the build to a specific scenario relevant to your organization: third-party risk, operational risk, regulatory exam management, or continuous monitoring with the CRI Profile and Advanced Risk module.

{% hint style="success" %}
**Checkpoint habit.** At the end of each phase, use the phase checkpoint page to verify your instance matches the expected state before moving forward. It takes fifteen minutes and prevents carrying forward configuration errors that compound in later phases.
{% endhint %}

## Key takeaways

* Always complete Foundations before Series 1 — the vocabulary foundation pays for itself immediately.
* Series 1 phases are sequentially dependent; follow them in order.
* CISOs and executives can get significant value from Foundations plus Phase 4 (Reporting) without doing the hands-on configuration phases.
* Internal auditors should prioritize the Three Lines of Defense and Phase 3 (Control Testing) pages.
* The Glossary and Regulatory Frameworks Overview are reference materials — keep them open in a separate tab throughout your reading.
