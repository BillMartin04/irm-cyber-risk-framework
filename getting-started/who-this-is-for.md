## Overview

This handbook is for risk, compliance, security, and ServiceNow professionals who need to build and operate a real Integrated Risk Management practice — not a slide deck version of one. The content is grounded in financial services and banking contexts where regulators, internal audit, and the board all have direct opinions about how risk management is structured and evidenced. If you are moving from spreadsheets to a platform, standing up the IRM module for the first time, or inheriting an existing implementation that needs a redesign, this handbook is written for you.

## Key concepts

* **GRC / IRM practitioners** — Risk analysts and GRC officers who own the risk register, run assessments, track issues and findings, and report to the Chief Risk Officer or CISO. This handbook gives you the platform depth to stop depending on spreadsheets and start producing defensible, real-time risk posture data.
* **ServiceNow administrators** — Admins who need to configure the IRM/GRC plugin, set up Risk Statements, Control Objectives, Indicators, Entities, and Profiles — and who want to understand the business logic behind each configuration decision before they build it.
* **Security architects and CISOs** — Leaders who must map the organization’s control framework to regulatory requirements and present risk posture to executives and the board. The framework coverage and reporting chapters are built for this audience.
* **Internal auditors and second-line risk officers** — Professionals in second- and third-line roles who need to understand how the IRM platform structures evidence, tracks control effectiveness, and supports assurance activities. Understanding the data model and workflow is critical for evaluating findings and issuing audit opinions.
* **Enterprise architects** — Architects designing an IRM operating model across multiple business units, geographies, or regulatory jurisdictions. The handbook’s IRM-specific design patterns and the Three Lines of Defense mapping provide a starting point for that design work.

## Who this is NOT for

This handbook does not cover general IT Service Management, Change Management, or ITOM on ServiceNow. It does not cover IT project or portfolio delivery. The scope is IRM and cyber risk: risk identification and assessment, control management, policy and compliance, regulatory framework mapping, and risk reporting. If you need ITSM fundamentals, start with ServiceNow’s own learning paths before returning here.

## What experience level is assumed?

Basic ServiceNow navigation is assumed — you should be able to log in, find a module, open a record, and submit a form. No scripting knowledge is required. The hands-on sections walk through configuration steps using the platform UI. Foundational GRC concepts — risk, control, policy, audit — are defined in the [GRC Terminology Glossary](../foundations/grc-terminology-glossary.md), so prior GRC expertise is helpful but not a hard prerequisite.

For regulatory and financial-services context: the banking and operational risk examples assume some familiarity with how regulated financial institutions are structured (lines of business, Risk function, Internal Audit, regulators), but every concept is explained in enough detail that professionals from other industries can follow along.

## Key takeaways

* This handbook targets practitioners — people who configure, operate, and are accountable for the IRM program, not just observers.
* Financial services context is used throughout, but the framework applies broadly to any regulated industry.
* No scripting or advanced ServiceNow experience is required; platform navigation basics are sufficient.
* The audience spans all Three Lines — first-line risk owners, second-line oversight, and third-line audit all have relevant content here.
* Prior GRC experience accelerates learning but is not a prerequisite; the Foundations section builds the shared vocabulary from scratch.
