## Overview

This community exists to support practitioners building and operating ServiceNow IRM programs. It works because participants engage in good faith — sharing real experience, asking genuine questions, and accepting constructive critique. The Code of Conduct defines the standards that make this possible. It applies to all community spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and any associated forum or chat channel.

## Key concepts

* **Constructive engagement** — Feedback on contributions, questions, or ideas should be specific, actionable, and respectful. Critique the content, not the person. "This control mapping is incorrect because X" is constructive; dismissive or personal comments are not.
* **Good-faith participation** — Contributions and questions should reflect genuine intent to improve the resource or learn from it. Promotional content, off-topic posts, or attempts to redirect community members to unrelated products or services are not permitted.
* **Expertise without gatekeeping** — This community includes practitioners at every level — from first-time IRM implementers to seasoned GRC architects. Expertise differences should produce helpful guidance, not condescension. A question that seems basic deserves a useful answer.
* **Accuracy over speed** — In a community focused on risk and compliance content, inaccurate information is a real harm — practitioners may rely on it. If you are unsure, say so. If you see inaccurate content, raise a correction politely with a specific citation or evidence.
* **Respectful disagreement** — Subject matter experts will sometimes disagree on approach, prioritization, or interpretation of a framework requirement. Disagreement is welcome and productive; it should be conducted with specific arguments and mutual respect.

## Expected Behaviors

* Provide specific, cited feedback when challenging existing content.
* Acknowledge when you are uncertain or when a question is outside your area of expertise.
* Give credit when building on someone else's work, question, or idea.
* Use inclusive language and treat all participants with professional courtesy.
* Follow the contribution workflow described in the How to Contribute page — do not bypass the review process.

## Unacceptable Behaviors

* Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory language of any kind.
* Posting inaccurate information without a good-faith basis and ignoring corrections.
* Promotional content, vendor marketing, or off-scope solicitations.
* Submitting content or code that has not been tested and misrepresenting it as validated.
* Sharing confidential client or employer information — all examples should be anonymized or hypothetical.

## Reporting and Enforcement

If you observe behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it to the maintainers via the GitHub repository's private contact channel (specified in the repository README). Reports are reviewed confidentially. Depending on severity, responses range from a private reminder to removal of content and, for repeated or severe violations, removal from the community.

Maintainers are not infallible. If you believe a moderation decision was incorrect, you may request a second review by a different maintainer through the same channel.

## Key takeaways

* The community's value depends on accurate, specific, good-faith content — both the Code of Conduct and the content standards reinforce this.
* Expertise differences should produce helpful guidance, not gatekeeping or condescension.
* Anonymize all examples — never share confidential client or employer information in any community space.
* Maintainers are accessible and responsive; bring concerns to them promptly rather than letting them escalate in public threads.
