## Overview

Enterprise Insider is the team-level tier for organizations running ServiceNow IRM at scale — multiple business units, regulatory domains, or subsidiaries. It provides everything in Architect Pro, plus multi-seat team access, enterprise deployment guides, and direct engagement opportunities for organizational implementations. It is designed for GRC teams, consulting practices, and technology organizations that need more than one practitioner to have access to the full resource library.

## Key concepts

* **Who it's for** — GRC teams at regulated institutions (banks, health systems, federal agencies) running a ServiceNow IRM program; ServiceNow consulting practices with multiple IRM-certified practitioners; enterprise architecture teams building a multi-domain IRM deployment across subsidiaries or organizational units; organizations sponsoring employee certification paths at scale.
* **Team access** — Enterprise Insider provides a configurable number of member seats under a single organizational account. Each seat holder gets full Architect Pro access — complete update set library, template library, CIS-RC prep bundle, and deep-dive documentation. Team members can be added or rotated as staff changes.
* **Organizational continuity** — Individual Architect Pro memberships are tied to individual accounts. Enterprise Insider ties the resource library to the organizational account, so when a team member departs, their access transitions to their replacement without content loss or re-procurement.

## What's Included

Everything in Architect Pro, plus:

* **Multi-seat team access** — Configurable seats (based on tier package) under a single organizational account.
* **Enterprise deployment guides** — Documentation covering multi-domain IRM architecture: separate Entity hierarchies for subsidiaries with consolidated enterprise reporting; scoped application design for organizations with distinct regulatory domains (e.g., a bank with both U.S. FFIEC and EU DORA obligations); multi-instance vs. single-instance deployment trade-offs.
* **Quarterly enterprise briefings** — Live sessions focused on enterprise-level topics: new ServiceNow IRM release features and their impact on running programs, regulatory developments affecting the IRM platform configuration, and anonymized case studies from enterprise deployments. Recorded and available post-session.
* **Private Q&A channel** — An Enterprise Insider-only discussion channel separate from the general community Q&A. Allows sensitive organizational questions to be raised without public disclosure.
* **Implementation review sessions** — On request, a scheduled review session where your team presents current implementation architecture and receives expert feedback. Useful before a major upgrade, platform re-architecture, or audit preparation period.
* **Priority issue response** — GitHub Issues and content gap reports raised by Enterprise Insider organizations receive a response within 48 hours and are given elevated roadmap priority when they reflect broad organizational need.

## Value for GRC Teams at Regulated Institutions

For a GRC team at a bank, health system, or federal agency, Enterprise Insider addresses the organizational-level challenges that individual memberships cannot:

* **Onboarding new team members** — When a new risk analyst or consultant joins the team, their immediate access to the full update set library and deep-dive documentation shortens ramp time from weeks to days.
* **Certification program support** — Organizations with ServiceNow IRM programs benefit from having multiple CIS-RC certified staff. Enterprise Insider's team access provides every team member with the prep bundle without individual purchase friction.
* **Consistency across team** — When multiple practitioners are building on the same platform, using shared update sets, diagrams, and templates produces a consistent configuration baseline — reducing the "everyone configured it differently" problem that plagues large IRM programs.
* **Enterprise architecture guidance** — The enterprise deployment guides address the design decisions that matter at scale — ones that are rarely discussed in standard certification courses or product documentation.
