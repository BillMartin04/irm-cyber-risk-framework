## Overview

Phase 3 is where theory becomes practice. In Phases 1 and 2 you built the foundation — Risk Register, Control library, Policy framework, Risk Assessments, and KRI/Indicator monitoring. Now you apply that foundation independently, tackling the scenarios that risk and compliance teams in financial services face every day: communicating posture to the board, automating responses to emerging risk signals, managing the sprawling web of third-party relationships, and producing the evidence trail that survives a regulatory examination.

For a bank or asset manager operating under OCC, Federal Reserve, or DORA oversight, these are not optional capabilities. Examiners expect real-time dashboards backed by structured data, not spreadsheets assembled the week before the exam. They expect documented vendor assessments with tiered criticality, evidence attached to controls at the time of testing — not reconstructed after the fact. Phase 3 builds exactly that operational discipline inside ServiceNow IRM.

## What Phase 3 covers

Four tightly connected topics make up this phase:

* **Risk Dashboards and Reporting** — Configuring Performance Analytics indicators, KRI threshold widgets, and executive-facing dashboards that give your Chief Risk Officer and board risk committee a live view of operational and cyber risk posture.
* **Automated Risk Responses** — Using ServiceNow Flow Designer to build automation that reacts when risk or control metrics breach thresholds: creating Issues, assigning remediation tasks, triggering notifications, and escalating along defined SLA chains.
* **Vendor and Third-Party Risk** — Managing the full VRM lifecycle: onboarding suppliers, tiering them by criticality, issuing assessment questionnaires, tracking responses, and surfacing vendor risk back into the enterprise Risk Register.
* **Audit and Evidence Collection** — Running Audit Management engagements end-to-end, attaching evidence to controls at test time, tracking findings to closure, and producing an immutable audit trail that regulators can verify.

## How to work through Phase 3

Each topic in this phase is designed to be completed independently — you have enough context from Phases 1 and 2 to tackle them in any order. That said, a natural sequence is dashboards first (so you can see your data), then automation (so the platform starts acting on that data), then vendor risk and audit (which extend the framework outward to third parties and examiners). Each page includes step-by-step guidance, real banking examples, and a video walkthrough.
