## Overview

Phase 1 lays the conceptual and navigational groundwork for everything that follows in this series. Before you can run a risk assessment or map controls to a regulatory framework, you need a clear mental model of what ServiceNow IRM is, how it structures the Three Lines of Defense, and how to move purposefully inside the platform. These four lessons build that foundation in sequence — from "what is this product?" to a direct comparison with the spreadsheet-and-email workflows that most banks are still trying to replace.

The phase is structured so that each lesson builds on the last. You will finish Phase 1 knowing why ServiceNow IRM exists, how your organization's risk and compliance roles translate into platform roles and record ownership, where every major record type lives in the UI, and what you gain — concretely — by moving off legacy tooling.

## What Phase 1 covers

| Lesson | Core question answered |
|---|---|
| What Is ServiceNow IRM | What are the core modules, and how do they relate to each other? |
| Three Lines of Defense in ServiceNow | Which platform roles and records belong to each line? |
| Navigating the IRM Module | Where do I find risks, controls, assessments, and policies? |
| IRM vs Traditional Tools | What does the platform do that a spreadsheet genuinely cannot? |

## Learning objectives

By the end of Phase 1 you will be able to:

- Describe the ServiceNow IRM application suite — Risk Management, Policy & Compliance, Audit Management, and their shared data model.
- Map the Three Lines of Defense to concrete platform roles, record ownership, and workflow hand-offs.
- Navigate the IRM workspaces, locate key record types (Risk Register, Controls, Assessments, Issues), and understand the role-based view design.
- Articulate three to five specific limitations of traditional GRC tooling that the ServiceNow platform resolves — with examples drawn from operational risk and regulatory exam management in banking.

## Who this phase is for

Phase 1 is written for practitioners who are new to ServiceNow IRM or who have used it in a limited capacity and want a structured foundation. It assumes familiarity with GRC concepts (risk registers, controls, compliance frameworks) but no prior ServiceNow experience is required. If you are coming from a Big Four audit background, a bank's first- or second-line risk function, or a compliance team that lives in spreadsheets, this phase speaks directly to your context.
