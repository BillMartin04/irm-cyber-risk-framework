## Overview

Phase 2 moves from conceptual understanding to hands-on execution. With the foundation from Phase 1 in place — the platform model, the Three Lines structure, and navigation — you are now ready to operate the core workflows that make up a working IRM program. These four lessons walk through the most important practitioner activities in ServiceNow IRM, each with step-by-step guidance and banking-specific examples.

Phase 2 covers the complete lifecycle of an IRM program's core cycles: scoping and running a risk assessment, running control attestations for compliance purposes, mapping your control library to industry frameworks including the FSSCC Cyber Risk Institute CRI Profile, and managing the issues and findings that flow from assessments and audits. By the end of this phase, you will have the skills to operate — not just observe — a ServiceNow IRM deployment at a bank or financial institution.

## What Phase 2 covers

| Lesson | Core skill developed |
|---|---|
| Risk Assessments Step-by-Step | Scope, run, score, and interpret a structured risk assessment |
| Compliance Assessments | Configure and execute control attestations; capture and link results |
| CRI Profile and Framework Mapping | Map your control library to CRI Profile, NIST CSF, and ISO 27001 |
| Issues and Findings Management | Convert gaps and findings into tracked, remediated, closed issues |

## Learning objectives

By the end of Phase 2 you will be able to:

- Execute a full risk assessment workflow in ServiceNow — from scoping an entity to interpreting residual scores against risk appetite.
- Configure and run a compliance assessment for a set of controls, capture attestation results, and link failures to risk records.
- Understand the FSSCC Cyber Risk Institute CRI Profile structure and map it to NIST CSF and ISO 27001 within the ServiceNow Policy & Compliance framework.
- Create, assign, track, and close issues and findings in ServiceNow, managing the complete remediation lifecycle from identification to verified closure.

## Who this phase is for

Phase 2 is for practitioners who are ready to work in ServiceNow IRM — second-line risk managers, compliance analysts, information security practitioners, and internal auditors who need to operate the platform, not just understand its architecture. The banking and financial-services examples are drawn from real program management scenarios: quarterly operational risk assessments, annual cyber risk attestations, OCC examination cycles, and audit finding remediation tracking.
