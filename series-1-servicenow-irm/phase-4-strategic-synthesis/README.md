## Overview

By the time you reach Phase 4, you have built a functioning ServiceNow IRM program: a Risk Register populated with risk statements, controls tested and evidence attached, KRIs monitored with automated responses, vendors assessed and tiered, and dashboards keeping leadership informed. The question Phase 4 asks is a harder one — is this program good, and is it getting better? Strategic synthesis means stepping back from individual records and configurations to evaluate the IRM capability as a whole: where does it sit on a maturity spectrum, where does it need to go, and how do you communicate that trajectory to the board, regulators, and the business?

For a financial institution, this is not an abstract exercise. Regulators — OCC, Federal Reserve, DORA, PRA — increasingly assess not just whether controls exist but whether the risk management function has the governance, tools, and culture to improve over time. A static, reactive program that has not evolved in three years is a finding waiting to happen. A program that can demonstrate measured maturity growth, linked to a documented roadmap and investment plan, is a program that earns examiner confidence.

## How to approach Phase 4

Phase 4 is less hands-on in the platform and more analytical. You will be using the data you have built in Phases 1–3 as inputs — looking at indicator trends, audit finding velocity, vendor risk posture, and control effectiveness rates to form an honest assessment of where the program stands. The output is a strategy document and roadmap that you can present to a board risk committee, share with an internal audit team, or use as the basis for a regulatory self-assessment.

Take the time to be honest in your maturity assessment. Overstating maturity is a credibility risk — examiners who probe deeper than the self-assessment will find the gap, and the finding is worse for having been misrepresented. An accurate Level 2 assessment with a credible roadmap to Level 3 is far stronger than an inflated Level 4 claim with no evidence to support it.
