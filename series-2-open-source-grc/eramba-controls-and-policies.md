## Overview

A risk register on its own only describes exposure. Controls are how you reduce it, and policies are how you set expectations across the organization. This page builds Eramba's control library, links controls to the risks from the previous page, and configures policy attestations so you can demonstrate that people have read and accepted their obligations.

## Building the control library

* **Import a control framework** — Start from a recognized baseline (ISO 27001 Annex A, NIST CSF, or CIS Controls) rather than inventing controls from scratch.
* **Define control owners and testing** — Each control gets an owner and a test procedure with a frequency. Eramba schedules the tests and tracks results.
* **Record control status** — Track whether each control is implemented, partially implemented, or planned, so gaps are visible.

## Linking controls to risks

* **Map controls to register risks** — Connect each control to the risks it mitigates. This is what lets you show inherent versus residual risk.
* **Demonstrate control value** — With the mapping in place, you can contrast exposure before controls against what remains after — the single most persuasive artifact for leadership and auditors.
* **Surface unmitigated risk** — Any risk with no linked control stands out immediately, driving remediation planning.

## Managing policies and attestations

* **Publish policies** — Load your policy set into Eramba and assign each policy to the relevant audience.
* **Run attestation campaigns** — Eramba sends attestation requests and tracks who has and has not acknowledged each policy.
* **Evidence the trail** — The attestation record becomes audit evidence that policies were communicated and accepted.

## Key takeaways

* Controls linked to risks are what turn a register into a defensible risk-treatment story.
* Starting from a recognized framework saves effort and improves auditor acceptance.
* Policy attestations in Eramba produce the communication evidence auditors ask for.
* The inherent-versus-residual view is the artifact that justifies control investment.
