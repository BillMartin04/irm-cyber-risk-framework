# The 9 Domains

> Once the Risk Universe is defined, it must be carved into domains that can actually be owned and managed.

The **9 Domains** are the taxonomy that segments the Risk Universe into distinct, manageable areas of risk. Each domain has its own scope, owners, controls, and measures — but all nine roll up into a single integrated view.

## Why nine domains

A single, monolithic "risk" view is unmanageable; too many categories become noise. Nine domains strike the balance: broad enough to cover the enterprise, focused enough that each has a clear owner and a coherent set of controls.

## The role of the domains in the framework

- **Segment** the Risk Universe into ownable areas
- **Map** to the 4 Lines of Defense (who defends each domain)
- **Anchor** the Intelligence (AI) layer (where automation and analytics apply)
- **Feed** the Maturity Model (each domain is scored 1–5)
