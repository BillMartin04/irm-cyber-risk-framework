# The 4 Lines of Defense

> Defining and segmenting risk is not enough — someone has to own it. The 4 Lines of Defense assign that ownership.

The **4 Lines of Defense (4LOD)** is the operating model that distributes responsibility for managing risk across the organization. It extends the traditional Three Lines model with a fourth line, reflecting how modern risk management actually works.

## The four lines

1. **First Line — Ownership.** The business units and operational teams that own and manage risk day to day.
2. **Second Line — Oversight.** Risk and compliance functions that set policy, monitor, and challenge the first line.
3. **Third Line — Assurance.** Internal audit, providing independent assurance to the board.
4. **Fourth Line — External assurance.** Regulators, external auditors, and independent third parties.

## How 4LOD fits the framework

- Each of the **9 Domains** is mapped to the lines that own, oversee, and assure it
- The **Intelligence (AI) layer** augments each line — automating first-line controls, sharpening second-line monitoring, and accelerating third-line assurance
- **Maturity** is assessed line by line, not just enterprise-wide
