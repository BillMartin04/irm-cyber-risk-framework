# The Risk Universe

> Series 2 opens by defining the whole problem space before structuring it. You cannot manage what you have not mapped.

The **Risk Universe** is the full, enterprise-wide set of risks an organization is exposed to — across technology, operations, compliance, third parties, and strategy. It is the outermost boundary of the framework: every domain, every line of defense, and every control ultimately traces back to something in this universe.

## Why start here

Most GRC programs start with tools or controls and work outward, which is why they end up fragmented. This framework starts from the opposite direction: define the universe first, then carve it into domains, then decide who defends each part, then apply intelligence, then measure maturity.

## What the Risk Universe covers

- The categories of risk in scope (and, deliberately, what is out of scope)
- How risks are identified, related, and rolled up to an enterprise view
- The shared taxonomy that the rest of the framework depends on

## Where this leads

The Risk Universe is segmented by the **9 Domains**, operated through the **4 Lines of Defense**, augmented by an **Intelligence (AI) layer**, and measured against a **5-Level Maturity Model**.
