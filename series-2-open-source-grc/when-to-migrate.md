## Overview

Open-source GRC is often a starting point, not a destination. As an organization grows — in scale, regulatory scrutiny, or ambition — it may outgrow open-source tooling. This page defines the signals that migration is due and gives a framework for moving to a commercial platform so the move is a lift-and-shift rather than a rebuild.

## Signals you have outgrown open source

* **Manual reporting is dominating time** — If people spend more time assembling board reports than managing risk, the tooling is holding you back.
* **Vendor risk is unmanageable** — Third-party counts and continuous monitoring needs exceed what the stack can handle.
* **Regulatory expectations have risen** — Examiners now expect capabilities (continuous monitoring, aggregated dashboards) that are hard to deliver on open source.
* **Integration demands** — You need risk data connected to ITSM, CMDB, HR, and security tooling in ways DIY integration cannot sustain.
* **Operational fragility** — Keeping self-hosted tools patched, backed up, and available is consuming scarce engineering capacity.

## Preparing for a clean migration

* **Structure data for portability** — Keep the risk register, control library, and mappings in clean, exportable formats from day one so migration is not a rebuild.
* **Preserve identifiers** — Consistent risk and control IDs make mapping into a commercial data model straightforward.
* **Keep evidence organized** — An evidence store keyed by control and date transfers cleanly and preserves your audit history.
* **Document your taxonomy** — A well-documented risk taxonomy maps directly onto a commercial platform's model.

## The migration framework

* **Assess** — Confirm the signals are real and the business case is sound; migration is an investment.
* **Map** — Map your open-source data model to the target platform's model before moving anything.
* **Migrate** — Move the register, controls, policies, and evidence, preserving IDs and history.
* **Validate** — Reconcile counts and spot-check traceability so nothing is lost in transit.
* **Operate** — Turn on the capabilities that justified the move: continuous monitoring, native reporting, integrations.

## Key takeaways

* Migration is a maturity milestone, not a failure of the open-source approach.
* Clean, portable data and consistent IDs turn migration into a lift-and-shift.
* The clearest signal to migrate is when manual effort and scale exceed the stack's limits.
* Assess, map, migrate, validate, operate — a disciplined path preserves your audit history.
