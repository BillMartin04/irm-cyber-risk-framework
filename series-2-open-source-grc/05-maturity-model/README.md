# The 5-Level Maturity Model

> Structure and intelligence are only useful if you can measure where you stand and where you are heading. The Maturity Model provides the scale.

The **5-Level Maturity Model** measures how advanced an organization's risk capability is across the Risk Universe, the 9 Domains, and the 4 Lines of Defense. It gives leaders a shared language for the current state, the target state, and the roadmap between them.

## The five levels

1. **Level 1 — Initial.** Ad hoc and reactive. Risk is handled case by case, with little consistency or documentation.
2. **Level 2 — Managed.** Basic processes exist and are repeatable, but they are siloed and largely manual.
3. **Level 3 — Defined.** Standardized processes and a common taxonomy are in place and applied consistently across domains.
4. **Level 4 — Quantified.** Risk is measured with data; controls are monitored continuously and reported with metrics.
5. **Level 5 — Optimized (Predictive).** Intelligence-driven and forward-looking. The organization anticipates risk and adapts controls automatically.

## How the model is used

- **Assess** each of the 9 Domains against the five levels
- **Assess** each of the 4 Lines of Defense independently
- **Target** a level per domain rather than a single enterprise number
- **Track** progress over time as the Intelligence (AI) layer matures

## Why five levels

Five levels is enough to show meaningful progression — from reactive (1) to predictive (5) — without becoming an academic exercise. Each step up is a concrete, defensible improvement in capability.
